Page 3 of 3 Our brains always have a goal, whether it’s to avoid a task, sneak a cookie from the jar, or complete a task with the
least amount of effort. The more explicit we are with our learners about the goals and incorporate their own goals, the more meaningful the learning experience will be, the
more purposeful the options available will be, and the less likely our brains will focus on competing goals.
Understanding these key facts about the learning brain not only helps educators in designing challenging, high quality learning opportunities, but they’re also incredibly
important for our learners to understand, as well. The more we understand about our own learning and how it happens, the further we advance toward the ultimate goal: becoming
expert learners.
