2. Literature review
2.1. Constructivist learning Constructivist learning is based on constructivist learning theory, which refers to student-centered learn-
ing. Learning is the process of constructing knowledge by students themselves through experience (Ahmad et al., 2015; Kugele, 2025). Therefore, students are active, thinking, constructing concepts or
doing other learning activities during the teaching-learning process. The role of the teacher is to facili- tate students in the process of constructing their knowledge to achieve optimal learning outcomes.
Rather than passively receiving information from teachers or the environment, students actively con- struct knowledge through personal experience, critical reflection and social interaction. Students are fully
involved in the learning process (Wu et al., 2022). Some of the principles underlying constructivist learning are emphasizing active student learning, building deep student understanding, emphasizing
self-reflection and metacognition as an integral part of learning, emphasizing collaboration, encouraging students to actively solve problems and teachers creating a learning environment that stimulates or
makes students learn optimally (Alsharif, 2014; B uy € ukduman & S € ¸irin, 2010; Gunduz & Hursen, 2015).
Constructivist learning in this study refers to a constructivist learning approach, which is a learning process that emphasizes students ’   active engagement in building knowledge through experience. Its
characteristics include being student-centered, facilitating students in constructing knowledge, relating learning to real-life contexts, enhancing motivation and utilizing various learning resources. In other
words, this constructivist learning approach serves as a general learning framework that highlights stu- dents ’   active role in constructing knowledge through teaching and learning interactions. The teacher ’ s
role is to create an environment that enables students to learn through the use of methods, media, learning resources, learning assessments and other instructional components.
The implementation of constructivist learning can be seen from the characteristics of the components of the teaching-learning process, in student learning activities, learning methods, learning media, learn-
ing resources or learning assessments. Learning activities emphasize student learning activities. Learning methods   use   multiple   learning   methods.   Learning   resources   also   use   multiple   learning   resources.
Likewise, from the learning media side, it also uses multiple learning media. Learning assessments use multiple learning assessments and emphasize authentic assessments. Student-centered learning strat-
egies (Anag un & An € ı lan, 2010; Demirci, 2010; Do et al., 2023; Epp et al., 2021; Rasiah, 2014). Using vari- ous   learning   facilities,   it   creates   an   environment   that   allows   students   to   actively   learn   optimally

### 2.2. Project-based learning
Project-based learning is related to strengthening collaboration, discipline, iterative and real abilities (Almulla, 2020). This learning model facilitates active, interactive and independent learning, while also
providing hands-on practice, thereby enabling students to gain meaningful learning experiences. It is expected to be effective in developing students ’   entrepreneurial competencies. However, until now, the
formulation of an effective project-based entrepreneurial education model has not been studied in depth.   Efforts   to   strengthen   students ’   entrepreneurial   competencies   are   largely   emphasized   in   the
content.
The project-based learning model emphasizes the activeness of learners in conducting exploration, analysis, synthesis, evaluation and obtaining other learning outcomes related to real life. In this study,
the project-based learning model refers to concrete instructional steps that use projects as the core of the learning process. Thus, this learning model is more specific, namely, directed toward the implemen-
tation of instructional steps in which the project serves as the central focus of learning. The students choose their project activities and conduct exploration, interpretation and evaluation to gain meaningful
experience. Students actively learn concretely and develop themselves. The students use project activ- ities as a learning tool to develop competencies, knowledge, skills and attitudes. Students not only gain
knowledge but also practical experience (I. B. Larsen, 2025; Pupik Dean et al., 2023).
The implementation of project-based learning can be seen from several characteristics of project- based learning, namely applying collaborative learning, emphasizing the practice of building, refining
and improving projects, producing products and emphasizing real-life (Almulla, 2020). Students create a framework, formulate problems, solve problems, collaborate, reflect and evaluate themselves continu-
ously and make changes. Project-based learning emphasizes contextual, inquiry and problem-solving.
Thus, it can strengthen students ’   problem-solving abilities, creativity and innovation. These are the main dimensions of students ’   entrepreneurial competencies.
### 2.3. Entrepreneurial competence
Entrepreneurial competency is a complex competency. Entrepreneurial competency refers to expertise, skills and abilities to achieve professional goals. Entrepreneurial competency includes professional know-
ledge, skills and attitudes. Entrepreneurial competencies also refer to creativity, the ability to make changes and generate new ideas (Tadeu & Paiva, 2015). Several dimensions must be possessed to dem-
onstrate entrepreneurial competency, including innovation ability, achievement orientation, establishing cooperation, making decisions, taking risks, solving problems and being visionary (Mart  ı nez-Mart  ı nez &
Ventura, 2020). It is also influenced by the individual ’ s attributes, including cognitive flexibility, self- efficacy and optimism (Alves & Yang, 2022). To improve it, a special and effective education model is
needed. The entrepreneurial education model is not only expected to improve the ability but also the willingness to do business (Cui et al., 2021). Education needs to be implemented intensively, not only
emphasizing knowledge but also practices. Teachers need to facilitate practical experiences, manage access that allows students practical competencies and encourage students to engage in entrepreneurial
practices   (E.   S.   H.   Larsen   et   al.,   2024).   There   is   a   significant   relationship   between   entrepreneurial
learning, business plan competitions and entrepreneurial practices (Lv et al., 2021).

On the other hand, the educational model that is also hypothesized to have a significant effect on students ’   entrepreneurial competence is the constructivist learning approach. The constructivist theory-
based learning model emphasizes student learning activity. Several research results show that construct- ivist theory-based learning can improve student creativity, critical thinking skills, problem-solving skills

2015; Tanjung et al., 2022). Performance-based learning applied by teachers strongly affects students ’ learning outcomes (Wiyono, Rasyad, et al., 2021). However, how high the influence of constructivist
learning on students ’   entrepreneurial competencies remains a question as well.
From a pedagogical perspective, the constructivist learning approach is a powerful, popular and engaging approach that emphasizes creating an effective learning environment for students. However,
its effectiveness is often debated. A systematic review of several studies has shown that the constructiv- ist learning approach has been proven effective for student learning (Arega & Hunde, 2025). However,
ongoing   research   is   needed   to   identify   the   most   effective   learning   strategies   for   implementation.
Constructivism   is   based   on   the   concept   that   individuals   actively   construct   their   own   knowledge.
Students are not recipients but creators of knowledge (Allen, 2022). Associated with Kant ’ s and Piaget ’ s theories, learning is understood as a process of connecting new knowledge with previously acquired
knowledge (Dennick, 2016). The teacher ’ s role is to facilitate and create an environment that encourages students to learn. To this end, implementation is carried out using a learning approach that utilizes mul-
tiple models, multiple methods, multiple media, multiple learning resources and multiple assessments for student-centered learning. The learning process can be conducted through cognitive constructivism,
social constructivism, inquiry-based learning, problem-based learning or discovery learning scenarios (EduCorpus, 2025).
Project-based learning is a learning model that refers to constructivist learning theory, characterized by specific steps that align with the scenario of problem-based learning. Its principles are student-
centered, with distinctive features such as being problem-oriented, emphasizing collaboration, utilizing projects as a means to learn subject matter and applying scientific practices ranging from research and
presentation of results to reflection (Auliyani et al., 2025; Markula & Aksela, 2022). Its main characteristic is the presence of projects as the center of student learning activities (Soia et al., 2025). The teacher ’ s
role is to implement project-based learning syntax, respond to student interests and provide attention, support and guidance to students (Morrison et al., 2021). The use of project-based learning has been
increasingly implemented in the learning process. However, its effectiveness is still often debated as well. Several studies have shown that project-based learning can enhance student engagement in the
learning process (Almulla, 2020). Nevertheless, the extent of its impact on students ’   final learning out- comes still requires further investigation (Guo et al., 2020).
The constructivist learning approach is general and can be conducted through some learning models.
Its implementation is carried out through a learning process that refers to the principles of constructivist learning theory, related to the use of multi-methods, instructional media, learning resources or assess-
ments. The project-based learning model is a specific model that is conducted through a certain syntax, starting from problem formulation, project design, schedule preparation, project monitoring and result
evaluation to product assessment. Based on some previous research, it is known that constructivist learning has an effect on creativity, critical thinking and student learning achievement (Savas et al.,
2012). Constructivist learning can improve students ’   skills in solving real-life problems and practical

2.5. Objectives of research Many previous studies have compared learning models based on constructivist learning theory with con-
ventional or traditional learning grounded in behaviorist learning theory (Mahbub & Beedle, 2025; Sokpheng & Meng, 2022). There has been limited research examining constructivist theory-based learn-
ing from the perspective of general learning approaches combined with specific instructional steps.
Most studies have focused on the processes and outcomes of learning in mathematics, science or social studies, with relatively few investigating its effectiveness in developing entrepreneurial competencies,
which represent a distinct domain of specialized competence (Bahufite et al., 2023; Chaiarwut et al., 2025; Fragkiadaki et al., 2021). This constitutes the novelty of the present study.
The purpose of this study is to describe the entrepreneurial learning model that is currently being implemented based on constructivist learning and project-based learning. In addition, this research also
tests the influence of the components of the constructivist learning process and project-based learning model on students ’   entrepreneurial competence. Theoretically, the results of this study greatly support
the development of learning theories, learning models and the development of students ’   entrepreneurial competence. Practically, the results of this study can provide information to lecturers about effective
learning models to develop students ’   entrepreneurial competence.

5. Discussions
Based on the research findings, it is evident that the quality of constructivist learning has a significant influence on students ’   entrepreneurial competencies. These findings are in line with and supported by
previous studies that highlight the positive impact of constructivist approaches on the development of entrepreneurial skills (Sioukas, 2023). Constructivist theory-based learning emphasizes the student in
learning. Students actively learn, face challenges, solve problems and carry out other learning activities.
On the other hand, mastering entrepreneurial competencies also requires students to be active in learn- ing. Through this activity, they can gain meaningful experience. The use of multiple learning resources
also contributes to the effectiveness of the learning process and outcomes (Wiyono et al., 2022; Wiyono, Supriyanto, et al., 2021).
The results of the second analysis revealed that the implementation of the Project-Based Learning model also had a significant effect on students ’   entrepreneurial competencies. These findings align with
previous studies, which have shown that the Project-Based Learning model can enhance students ’   inter- est and enthusiasm in learning entrepreneurship. The project-based learning model also encourages stu-
dents to produce business products (Santoso et al., 2023). Project-based learning models also have an impact on entrepreneurial enthusiasm, entrepreneurial literacy and entrepreneurial readiness (Yulastri
et al., 2023). Thus, the results of this study strengthen several previous research findings. If it is exam- ined further from the coefficient values that are obtained, it does not show too much difference. The
gamma coefficient of the constructivist learning quality variable obtained a coefficient of 0.27, while the project-based learning model obtained 0.10. The coefficient shows a little difference. The effect coeffi-
cient of constructivist learning is higher than the project-based learning model. This is because the project-based learning model is one of the models based on the constructivist theory. This is the novelty
of the research. If analyzed more deeply, the implementation level of project-based learning is still rela- tively low, and the model components, which tend to show a strong influence, are those that are active,
interactive, collaborative, productive and multidisciplinary, while those that have not shown action, for example, in planning, show less strong influence. Constructivist learning covers all parts of the teaching-
learning components, while the project-based learning model occurs at certain learning stages.
The characteristics of constructivist theory-based learning emphasize students ’   activeness. This is also the main characteristic of the project-based learning model. When it is examined from the constructivist
learning components, all components starting from the use of multi-models, methods, learning resour- ces, instructional media, emphasizing the improvement of knowledge, skills, attitudes, motivation and
creativity, activeness, learning interactions, use of information technology, fulfillment needs, fulfillment characteristics and achievement of goals have a significant influence on students ’   entrepreneurial com-
petence. Of the several components, the highest component is strengthening creativity. The reason is that it is the main characteristic that is contained in entrepreneurial competence (Karami et al., 2025).
If it is reviewed from the project-based learning components, all components starting from imple- menting the project-based learning model, formulating research problems, designing projects, making
schedules, working on projects, doing collaboration, cross-disciplinary knowledge, presenting results, producing products, evaluating results, conducting reflections, conducting authentic assessments and
the use of ICT all have a significant influence on students ’   entrepreneurial competencies. The relatively high ones are producing products, conducting reflection, conducting authentic assessments and the use
of ICT. This is related to the existing conditions; producing products is the main characteristic of entre- preneurial activities, while conducting reflection and authentic assessments is the process that assesses
students ’   real competencies, and the use of ICT is in line with the existing developments, which today ’ s entrepreneurial activities also require the use of information technology. Therefore, it has a strong influ-
ence on students ’   entrepreneurial competence.
This study ’ s findings strengthen the existing theory that constructivist learning improves students ’ problem-solving abilities (Kumar Shah, 2019). Project-based learning models can improve students ’   criti-
cal thinking skills (Yuliana & Mustadi, 2023). Project-based learning models emphasize collaboration and

Both learning implementations are based on constructivist learning theory. Thus, the use of constructiv- ist learning theory has a significant influence on students ’   entrepreneurial competencies. The use of gen-

through other learning models, which refer to the principles of constructivist learning theory, especially emphasizing the activeness and interaction of student learning. In addition, other learning components
are essential to be applied, namely the fulfillment of student needs, strengthening student creativity, evaluation and reflection of the learning process and results, and the steps of the learning model are
### also very important to be applied optimally.
The results of this study also indicate that the use of learning system components based on con- structivist theory, which include the use of multiple methods, multiple models, multiple media, multiple
learning resources, emphasis on student activeness and learning interaction, multiple assessments and the achievement of multiple student competencies, has a stronger influence compared to the specific
steps in the project-based learning model. While the project-based learning model is effective in improv- ing student competencies, it is challenging to implement and requires a relatively long time (Evenddy
### et al., 2023; Sumarni, 2024).

learning performance. However, the utilization of these resources does not always prove to be a consist- ent predictor of learning performance (Harder, 2025). This depends on students ’   active use of them. The
use of information technology-based learning resources also impacts students ’   learning processes and outcomes (Wiyono et al., 2022). However, many students also do not have sufficient mastery in using
search engines to obtain technology-based literature sources (Wiyono, Valdez, et al., 2021). The results of other studies show that the use of mixed learning methods is highly effective in learning (Shirani
Bidabadi et al., 2016). Authentic assessment can enhance students ’   academic performance and employ- able   skills   as   well   (Vlachopoulos   &   Makri,   2024).   In   addition,   other   research   findings   suggest   that
problem-based learning is effective in facilitating student learning. However, it remains unclear which specific components influence students ’   learning (Yew & Goh, 2016). Thus, the findings of this study
strengthen and expand upon previous research. On the one hand, this study is consistent with the results of previous studies, but on the other hand, it develops and discovers new findings. Not all learn-
ing models that refer to constructivist learning theory are effective for every course. They need to be adjusted to the characteristics of the subject matter and the competencies to be achieved. In a learning
scenario, there are also essential components of learning models that have been proven to significantly influence the learning process and outcomes, such as in project-based learning through its instructional
steps, the process of producing a project, authentic assessment and reflection. Therefore, these compo-
### nents need to be emphasized in the instructional process.

6. Conclusions
Based on the results of the analysis, it can be concluded that the constructivist theory-based learning model has a significant effect on students ’   entrepreneurial competence. The higher the intensity of using
the constructivist theory-based learning model, the higher its effect on students ’   entrepreneurial compe- tence. This strengthens the existing theory. The characteristics of constructivist learning emphasize the
activeness and interaction of the students. It is not only increasing knowledge, but also attitudes, skills and even creativity. Entrepreneurial competencies also contain independent characters, risk-taking, fac-
ing challenges, being innovative and working hard, which also emphasize the activeness and creativity of the students. Therefore, constructivist theory-based learning has a significant influence on students ’
entrepreneurial competence.
The results of the study also showed that the project-based learning model had a significant effect on students ’   entrepreneurial competence. This finding strengthens the existing theory. The characteris-
tics of project-based learning are centered on the students. The learning process encourages students to find their problems, design the projects, conduct the project, collaborate, produce products, reflect and
evaluate authentically. Finally, the project-based learning model instills the main components of entre- preneurial competence, namely, producing business work. Therefore, the project-based learning model
has a significant effect on students ’   entrepreneurial competence.
Components of the constructivist learning process correlate with students ’   entrepreneurial compe- tence. The highest component is increasing students ’   creativity. This shows that creativity is the main
competence of entrepreneurship. The components of the project-based learning process also correlate with students ’   entrepreneurial competence. This shows that the theoretical basis of the project-based
learning model has similarities with constructivist learning. Of the existing components, authentic assess- ment shows the highest correlation coefficient. This shows that to improve entrepreneurial competence,
it is necessary to do it through authentic assessment. Entrepreneurial competence cannot only be meas- ured through the cognitive domain, but must be demonstrated by students ’   real competence, which
includes the realm of knowledge, affective and skills or psychomotor.
